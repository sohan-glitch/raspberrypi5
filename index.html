Raspberry Pi 5

The evolution of the tiny computer—reborn with desktop-level power.

About Raspberry Pi 5

Released in late 2023, the Raspberry Pi 5 marks a major leap in the series, featuring new architecture, better performance, and enhanced connectivity.

Processor & Graphics

Powered by a Broadcom BCM2712 quad-core ARM Cortex-A76 @ 2.4 GHz and VideoCore VII GPU—up to 3× faster CPU and 2× faster graphics than Pi 4.

Memory Options

Available with 4 GB or 8 GB LPDDR4X RAM, delivering greater bandwidth and efficiency for multitasking and modern workloads.

Storage & Boot

Supports high-speed microSD and NVMe SSD via PCIe 2.0 x1 lane – finally letting you boot from blazing-fast drives.

Connectivity

Dual 4K HDMI outputs (60 Hz), 2× USB 3.0, 2× USB 2.0, Gigabit Ethernet, and Wi-Fi + Bluetooth 5.0 for seamless networking.

Software Support

Runs Raspberry Pi OS (Bookworm, 64-bit Debian base) with full desktop interface, plus Ubuntu Server, Kali Linux and others.

GPIO & Expansion

40-pin GPIO header compatible with previous Pi boards + PCIe connector for SSD adapters and expansion cards.

Cooling & Thermals

Higher power means more heat—active cooling is recommended. Official cooling kit keeps temps below 60 °C under load.

Performance Leap

Benchmarks show 2–3× speed gain over Pi 4. Can handle Chromium, VS Code, and light AI tasks without lag.

Power Supply

Requires a USB-C 5 V / 5 A adapter to feed the beast while driving dual monitors and USB peripherals.

Build & Design

Maintains the classic credit-card form factor but introduces a new power button and improved board layout for airflow.

Networking

Gigabit Ethernet through the RP1 chip brings true 1 Gb/s speed. Dual-band Wi-Fi 5 ensures low latency for IoT and media.

Multimedia

Dual 4K display support @ 60 Hz, HEVC video decode, and HDR playback make it a tiny but mighty media center.

Use Cases

Perfect for DIY servers, robotics, smart-home projects, education, and retro gaming stations with emulators up to PS2 level.

Pricing

Roughly $60 (4 GB) and $80 (8 GB) – affordable for the power it delivers, though accessories add to total cost.

Community & Future

A massive community drives continuous support and open-source projects that extend the Pi’s life for years ahead.

Conclusion

Raspberry Pi 5 bridges the gap between maker boards and mini-PCs – faster, smarter, still tiny.
